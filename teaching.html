Teaching

As an educator, I am committed to fostering inclusive, discussion-driven classrooms where students engage deeply with historical and contemporary issues in labor, race, and social justice.

Courses Taught

  • ILRGL 1100 – Introduction to U.S. Labor History (Teaching Assistant, Fall 2024)
  • African American and Latinx Histories (Teaching Assistant, Spring 2025)

Teaching Philosophy

My pedagogy centers on empowering students to critically analyze systems of power and develop the tools to imagine alternatives. I incorporate primary sources, community narratives, and collaborative assignments to cultivate both historical empathy and active civic engagement.

Mentorship and Community Engagement

I view teaching as an extension of organizing — grounded in care, curiosity, and collective growth. I support students from underrepresented backgrounds and mentor those pursuing research on labor, migration, and racial justice.
