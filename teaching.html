Teaching Philosophy

My teaching centers on creating welcoming, rigorous environments where students connect the history of labor with contemporary realities—through collaborative discussion, critical debate, and creative assignments, I empower learners to see their own role in shaping history.

Teaching Experience

Fall 2024
Introduction to US Labor History (ILRGL 1100)
Cornell University · Teaching Assistant for Prof. Paul Ortiz
Discussion Sections: Fridays 10:10am & 11:15am
Office Hours: Mondays 1:30-3:00pm

Explores working-class cultures, labor struggles, and the quest for dignity in US history from colonial period to present. Emphasized peer collaboration, critical debates, and connecting historical patterns to contemporary issues.

Course Syllabus (PDF)

Section Innovations & Teaching Methods:
  • Active Discussion: Weekly quizzes and thought-provoking activities to anchor key themes
  • Peer Review Workshops: Structured feedback sessions on synthesis essays with focus on evidence integration
  • Collaborative Timeline Creation: Group activities analyzing historical events and connecting themes across readings
  • Contemporary Connections: Weekly "Contemporary Thought Pieces" linking historical content to current labor issues
  • Critical Debates: Structured debates on topics like "The Role of Black Workers in Labor History"
  • Creative Assignments: Oral history reflections, timeline presentations, and thematic analysis projects
Weekly Section Highlights:
  • The Slave Ship & Labor Resistance: Analysis of Marcus Rediker's work, connecting transatlantic slavery to modern labor exploitation
  • Lowell Factory Girls & Black Folk: Group activities exploring gender, class, and organizing in 19th century America
  • Battle for Homestead Simulation: Role-playing debates and peer review
