Teaching Philosophy

My teaching centers on creating welcoming, rigorous environments where students connect the history of labor with contemporary realities—through collaborative discussion, critical debate, and creative assignments, I empower learners to see their own role in shaping history.

Weekly & Module Highlights

Sample: The Slave Ship & Labor Resistance
Multimedia discussion, primary source debate, and connecting Marcus Rediker's research to modern-day migrant labor.
Activities & Assignments:
  • Primary source analysis of ship manifests and slave narratives
  • Multimedia presentation combining historical documents with modern labor conditions
  • Group debate on capitalism, slavery, and contemporary labor exploitation
  • Creative timeline connecting Atlantic slave trade to current migrant labor
Learning Objectives:
  • Understand connections between historical and modern labor systems
  • Develop skills in primary source interpretation
  • Practice collaborative discussion and debate techniques
Women's Labor – Lowell Factory Girls
Used documentary film, class discussion on industrialization, and group timeline assignments to explore women's agency.
Activities & Assignments:
  • Documentary film analysis with guided discussion questions
  • Collaborative timeline creation on women's labor organizing
  • Role-play exercise simulating factory worker organizing meetings
  • Comparative essay on 19th century and contemporary women's labor issues
Key Themes:
  • Gender, class, and industrial capitalism
  • Women's agency in labor organizing
  • Economic independence and social constraints
Homestead Strike Debates
Peer-reviewed debate on labor ideology, role-played advisory committee deliberations, built skills in evidence-based argument.
Debate Structure:
  • Students assigned roles: workers, management, government officials, community members
  • Peer review of position papers before debate
  • Structured deliberation with evidence-based arguments
  • Reflection essays on multiple perspectives and historical complexity
Skills Developed:
  • Research and evidence evaluation
  • Public speaking and argumentation
  • Understanding multiple historical perspectives
