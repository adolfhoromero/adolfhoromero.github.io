Teaching Philosophy

My teaching centers on creating welcoming, rigorous environments where students connect the history of labor with contemporary realities—through collaborative discussion, critical debate, and creative assignments, I empower learners to see their own role in shaping history.

Courses Taught

Introduction to US Labor History (ILRGL 1100)
Cornell University · Fall 2024
Teaching Assistant to Prof. Paul Ortiz · Fridays 10:10am & 11:15am
Explores working-class cultures, labor struggles, and the quest for dignity in US history. Emphasized peer-reviewed debate, collaborative timelines, and creative assignments.
Syllabus (PDF)
Section Innovations:
  • Peer review & feedback workshops on synthesis essays
  • Collaborative history timeline creation
  • Debates on race, gender, and solidarity in labor movements
  • Oral history activities and student-centered analysis
  • Contemporary Thought Pieces linking past to news
Sample Weekly Highlights:
  • Rediker’s Slave Ship: Critical discussion on labor, capital, and resistance
  • Lowell Factory Girls/Black Folk: Group activities exploring gender, class, and organizing
  • Homestead Strike Simulation: Structured debates and peer review
  • Creative Assignments: Timeline presentations, thematic reflections
