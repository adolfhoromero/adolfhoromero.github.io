About My Journey

As a first-generation student from a working-class family in Chicago, I learned early on about struggle and solidarity. Growing up, I saw my parents working long hours in factories under difficult conditions, which instilled in me a personal commitment to fighting for social and economic justice. Those experiences not only shaped my identity but also inspired me to uplift marginalized voices and foster unity in my community.

Now, as a Ph.D. student in Industrial and Labor Relations at Cornell University, I carry those values into my research and activism. My work focuses on the intersections of labor, race, and community. As the first in my family to pursue a doctorate, I strive to honor my community through my scholarship and to bridge academic research with grassroots organizing. I believe in the power of education and collective action to create meaningful change, and I remain grounded in the principles of solidarity and the working-class experiences that have guided me from the start.
