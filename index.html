About My Journey

As a first-generation Mexican and Salvadorean from Humboldt Park, Chicago, I witnessed the challenges of working-class life and the strength found in solidarity. My experiences, rooted in family and community, drive my commitment to advancing social and economic justice for workers and migrants.

Today, as a rising PhD student in Cornell's School of Industrial and Labor Relations, my research builds on a decade of community-engaged projects from Florida to New York. Partnering with organizations, worker centers, and researchers, I strive to amplify marginalized voices—not as an outside expert, but as a collaborator and advocate working alongside those most affected.

My research is ethnographic, bilingual, and humble—focused on learning from those I serve and prioritizing participant dignity, confidentiality, and agency. I observe, listen, and support the collective power that emerges from shared leadership, organizing, and advocacy.
