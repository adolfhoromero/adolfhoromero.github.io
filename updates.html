Field notes & public work

Updates

A selective professional record—not a social-media feed. These entries document research, training, and community-engaged work, with the date and my role made explicit.

Role · Institute participant June 1–5, 2026 · Baltimore, Maryland

Johns Hopkins Racial Politics Summer Institute

Selected with scholarship support for an intensive graduate institute on racial politics, democratic backsliding, authoritarianism, and institutional power.

The institute helped me connect racial formation and migration politics to the institutions that organize labor, citizenship, and democratic participation.

Role · Presenter May 28–31, 2026 · Minneapolis, Minnesota

LERA 78th Annual Meeting

I presented “Is Ithaca Still a Union Town?” at the Labor and Employment Relations Association Annual Meeting.

The research examines worker power, local labor repertoires, and uneven regeneration in Tompkins County from 1984 to 2026.

Role · Presenter May 7, 2026 · Ithaca, New York

Cornell ILR Graduate Student Symposium

I presented “Field Generated Power Resources in Decentralized Labor Ecosystems” at the Global Labor and Work Department’s Graduate Student Symposium.

The presentation examined how worker centers and community organizations generate power through relationships, leadership development, and locally rooted institutions beyond the conventional union workplace.

Role · Safety-patrol participant January 30, 2026 · Ithaca, New York

Community safety and immigrant rights

I participated in a community safety patrol supporting immigrant neighbors, working alongside Indivisible, No Más Lágrimas, Ana Ortiz, and other local partners.

My role was supportive: helping maintain a safer public space while community leaders and families made their own demands visible.

Role · Participant and doctoral researcher August 18–21, 2025 · Austin, Texas

Urban Ethnography Lab Summer School

Hosted by the Urban Ethnography Lab at the University of Texas at Austin, the summer school treated ethnography as the disciplined construction of evidence rather than neutral data collection.

We worked through in-depth interviewing, interaction and conversation analysis, qualitative data analysis, memoing, evidentiary warrants, deviant cases, epistemological vigilance, and non-extractive ethics—including how researchers return knowledge to the communities that make the work possible.

Role · Participant and Cornell graduate researcher May 5–9, 2025 · Gozo, Malta

Queen Mary–Cornell Global Migration Spring School

At Queen Mary University of London’s Malta campus, I joined a cross-disciplinary cohort examining migration through geography, history, law, public health, and policy.

The program sharpened how I connect migrant labor pathways and displacement to urban governance, borders, and the institutional production of exclusion.
