Labor, Migration, Community Power, and Democratic Practice

My research examines how workers and migrant communities build power, dignity, and collective capacity in contexts where formal labor protections are weak, exclusionary, or absent. I combine qualitative interviews, oral history, archival work, and community engaged research to study worker centers, alternative labor institutions, and local governance.

Worker Centers and Alternative Labor Institutions

I examine how worker centers and community based organizations support workers excluded from traditional labor protections. I focus on how these institutions navigate legal precarity, limited resources, and internal diversity while fostering leadership, political participation, and collective action.

Migration, Race, and Urban Governance

I situate labor struggles within migration governance, racialization, and the urban political economy. I study how cities mediate exclusionary federal and state policies through local enforcement practices, nonprofit governance, and informal regulation of work.

Oral History, Memory, and Community Knowledge

Oral history is both a method and an ethical commitment in my scholarship. I document worker experience, preserve community memory, and challenge narratives that marginalize working class and migrant voices. I treat interviewees as historical agents and collaborators.

Labor, Democracy, and Dignity

Across these projects, my work is animated by questions of democracy and dignity. I ask how workers create meaningful forms of participation and belonging in settings marked by economic precarity, racial inequality, and political exclusion.

Methods and Approach

My research draws on qualitative interviews, oral history, archival research, and community engaged methods. I work closely with worker organizations and community partners, and I remain attentive to the ethical and political dimensions of knowledge production. Analytically, my work engages labor studies, critical migration scholarship, and political economy with sustained attention to power, race, and institutional mediation.
